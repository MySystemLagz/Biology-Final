Parasitism

A symbotic relationship between two organisms in which one benefits while the other is harmed.

Examples include

Commensalism

A symbotic relationship between two organisms in which one benefits while the other is not affected.

Mutualism

A symbotic relationship between two organisms in which both organisms benefit.

Predation

The lethal act of eating other organisms.

Herbivory

The act of eating other organisms, not always lethal.

Trophic Levels

The energy transfer between producers, primary consumers, secondary consumers, tertiary consumers, quaternary consumers (optional), and decomposers. Approximately 10% of energy goes up each level because animals lose the other 90% of that energy through metabolic heat.
